Highwire

In 2012, I co-designed and prototyped an interactive exhibit for the Taubman School of Architecture. This exhibit is currently on display at the North Campus Research Complex in Ann Arbor, Michigan.
Interaction Design

Designed in two weeks, Highwire aims to create an atmosphere that reflects the conversations and activity nearby, while reflecting the collaborative nature of the research complex.

Structure

A structure was designed by students from the Taubman School of Architecture. My partners and I designed the interactive installation to fit within this constraint.

Prototyping

The exhibit was prototyped using Arduino, LED strips, motion sensors, and microphones. After it was prototyped, the design was scaled across the entire exhibit. My contributions were in design and prototyping. The full project was implemented after my departure.
