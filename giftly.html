Giftly

In 2012, I worked with 3 other students to do user research and usability analyses for Giftly, a gift card startup. Our goals were to evaluate its usability and propose new ways to attract users.
Research Summary

We ran a full gamut of studies, surveys, and analyses. As a result, we discovered a number of technical and design issues, as well as provided recommendations for improvement.

Interaction Map

Our first step in understanding Giftly was conducting a thorough inventory of all major interactions on the site.

Personas & Scenarios

Based on interviews which focused on gift-giving behavior, we developed 3 personas and scenarios that described Giftly's target users.

Comparative Analysis

We studied comparable services to Giftly to better understand their domain, as well as discover patterns in online gifting technology.

Heuristic Evaluation

Using Jakob Nielsen's 10 Heuristics as a starting point, we conducted a Heuristic Evaluation of Giftly's product. Each of us completed an individual analysis, and we later consolidated our results.

Surveys

Building on our other data, we distributed a survey to roughly 100 users in Giftly's target market in order to evaluate how well user needs are being met, as well as discover potential improvements to the product.

Usability Tests

We conducted 10 usability tests on users in Giftly's target market. These tests revealed 11 key usability gaps and potential improvements. We provided recommendations for improvement to Giftly.
